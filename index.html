Foundation question-bank priorities

Where the strongest coverage should sit

The Foundation syllabus is not evenly weighted. The library should deliberately contain far more high-quality practice questions for practices, followed by processes and people.

60% Practices

Highest priority: business case, organizing, plans, quality, risk, issues and progress. Build strong coverage of purposes, management products, roles, techniques and interactions.

15% Processes

Purpose, objectives, activities, decision points, responsibilities and the flow from starting up through closure.

14% People

Roles, leadership, teams, stakeholders, communication and organizational change.

8% Principles

Recognition, purpose, consequences of not applying them and tailoring without breaking the method.

3% Core concepts

Projects, outputs, outcomes, benefits, performance targets, delivery approaches and project context.

Next content priority: deepen the seven practices evenly, especially Quality, Issues and Progress, then add balanced process questions that test who does what, when, and using which management product.
